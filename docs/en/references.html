References¶

The bibliography cited throughout the project. Each entry gives the official page (DOI/publisher) and, where a free PDF can be downloaded, a PDF link. These match the footnotes at the bottom of each algorithm page.

Implemented algorithms¶

  • BFS — Moore, E. F. (1959). "The shortest path through a maze." Proc. Int. Symp. on the Theory of Switching, Harvard University Press, 285–292.
  • Independent discovery for circuit routing: Lee, C. Y. (1961). "An algorithm for path connections and its applications." IRE Trans. Electronic Computers, EC-10(3), 346–365. — DOI
  • Dijkstra — Dijkstra, E. W. (1959). "A note on two problems in connexion with graphs." Numerische Mathematik, 1, 269–271. — DOI
  • A* — Hart, P. E., Nilsson, N. J., & Raphael, B. (1968). "A Formal Basis for the Heuristic Determination of Minimum Cost Paths." IEEE Trans. Systems Science and Cybernetics, 4(2), 100–107. — DOI
  • Correction: Hart, Nilsson & Raphael (1972). SIGART Newsletter, 37, 28–29. — DOI
  • Weighted A*: Pohl, I. (1970). "Heuristic search viewed as path finding in a graph." Artificial Intelligence, 1(3–4), 193–204. — DOI
  • RRT — LaValle, S. M. (1998). "Rapidly-exploring random trees: A new tool for path planning." Technical Report TR 98-11, Iowa State University. — PDF
  • Kinodynamic: LaValle, S. M., & Kuffner, J. J. (2001). "Randomized kinodynamic planning." Int. J. of Robotics Research, 20(5), 378–400. — DOI · PDF
  • RRT* — Karaman, S., & Frazzoli, E. (2011). "Sampling-based algorithms for optimal motion planning." Int. J. of Robotics Research, 30(7), 846–894. — DOI · PDF (arXiv)
  • Fast-RRT — Wu, Z., Meng, Z., Zhao, W., & Wu, Z. (2021). "Fast-RRT: A RRT-Based Optimal Path Finding Method." Applied Sciences, 11(24), 11777. — PDF (open access) · DOI

Planned algorithms¶

  • RRT-Connect — Kuffner, J. J., & LaValle, S. M. (2000). "RRT-Connect: An efficient approach to single-query path planning." Proc. IEEE ICRA, 995–1001. — DOI
  • Informed RRT* — Gammell, J. D., Srinivasa, S. S., & Barfoot, T. D. (2014). "Informed RRT*: Optimal sampling-based path planning focused via direct sampling of an admissible ellipsoidal heuristic." Proc. IEEE/RSJ IROS, 2997–3004. — DOI · PDF (arXiv)
  • DWA — Fox, D., Burgard, W., & Thrun, S. (1997). "The dynamic window approach to collision avoidance." IEEE Robotics & Automation Magazine, 4(1), 23–33. — DOI
  • Pure Pursuit — Coulter, R. C. (1992). "Implementation of the Pure Pursuit path tracking algorithm." CMU-RI-TR-92-01, Carnegie Mellon University. — PDF
  • VFH — Borenstein, J., & Koren, Y. (1991). "The vector field histogram — fast obstacle avoidance for mobile robots." IEEE Trans. Robotics and Automation, 7(3), 278–288. — DOI
  • Prioritized planning — Erdmann, M., & Lozano-Pérez, T. (1987). "On multiple moving objects." Algorithmica, 2, 477–521. — DOI
  • CBS — Sharon, G., Stern, R., Felner, A., & Sturtevant, N. R. (2015). "Conflict-based search for optimal multi-agent pathfinding." Artificial Intelligence, 219, 40–66. — DOI

Background¶

  • Occupancy grid — Elfes, A. (1989). "Using occupancy grids for mobile robot perception and navigation." Computer, 22(6), 46–57. — DOI
  • Textbook — LaValle, S. M. (2006). Planning Algorithms. Cambridge University Press. — free online
  • MPC textbook — Rawlings, J. B., Mayne, D. Q., & Diehl, M. (2017). Model Predictive Control: Theory, Computation, and Design (2nd ed.). Nob Hill Publishing.

Link legend: DOI = official publisher page (some paywalled). PDF = free download from an author/open-access/preprint page. Paywalled journal papers list DOI only.
