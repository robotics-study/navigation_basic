Algorithms¶

Detailed pages for each implemented algorithm. Every page covers the theoretical background (with original-paper footnotes), pseudocode, properties (completeness, optimality, complexity), parameter declarations, emitted trace events, demo visualizations (GIF + intermediate-progress PNGs), and measured metrics.

Global planning — Implemented¶

Algorithm Year Approach Completeness Optimality Original paper
BFS 1959 uninformed search complete fewest edges (optimal under unit cost) Moore 1
Dijkstra 1959 uninformed search complete cost-optimal Dijkstra 2
A* 1968 informed search complete cost-optimal (admissible h) Hart, Nilsson & Raphael 3
D* Lite 2002 dynamic replanning complete optimal for the belief (incremental repair) Koenig & Likhachev 5
Theta* 2007 any-angle search complete any-angle (not grid-optimal) Nash, Daniel, Koenig & Felner 4
RRT 1998 sampling probabilistically complete non-optimal (feasible) LaValle 6
RRT* 2011 sampling probabilistically complete asymptotically optimal Karaman & Frazzoli 7
PRM 1996 sampling (roadmap) probabilistically complete non-optimal (fixed radius) Kavraki et al. 8
PRM* 2011 sampling (roadmap) probabilistically complete asymptotically optimal Karaman & Frazzoli 7
FMT* 2015 sampling (batch marching) probabilistically complete asymptotically optimal Janson et al. 9
BIT* 2015 sampling (batch + informed) probabilistically complete almost-surely asymptotically optimal Gammell et al. 10
Fast-RRT 2021 sampling (RRT* variant) probabilistically complete near-optimal (faster convergence than RRT*) Wu et al. 11

Lineage note. RRT* branches two ways. The roadmap/batch-optimal family PRM → PRM* → FMT* → BIT* attains asymptotic optimality through radius policies and batch search, while Fast-RRT is a variant descending from RRT* (an RRT* variant, 2021) that adds shortcutting to speed up convergence. The sources stay in a flat directory layout, but in lineage terms Fast-RRT is a descendant of RRT*.

Running the Demos¶

All demos take the same CLI arguments in both languages:

# Python
python python/demos/demo_<algo>.py \
  --map maps/grid/<map>.yaml --scenario maps/scenarios/<map>_s1.yaml \
  --params configs/global_planning/<algo>.yaml --trace out/<algo>.jsonl \
  [--seed N] [--connectivity 4|8]

# C++ (identical arguments)
./cpp/build/demos/demo_<algo> --map ... --scenario ... --params ... --trace ...

# visualization: animated GIF + mid-search PNG snapshots
python tools/viz/replay.py out/<algo>.jsonl --gif out/<algo>.gif --snapshots out/snaps/
